# Business Memo: Customer Behavior Insights Before Retention Strategy

## Overview
An exploratory analysis was conducted across customer transactions, engagement behavior, support activity, refund behavior, campaign exposure, and churn labels. The objective was to identify real behavioral patterns linked to churn risk.

---

## Key Findings from the Data

### 1. Strong Skew in Purchase Behavior
Order frequency analysis shows that most customers place only a few orders, while a small group is highly active.

### Business Insight:
Customer activity is heavily concentrated in a small segment, meaning retention of high-frequency users is critical for revenue stability.

---

### 2. Revenue Concentration Among Few Customers
Gross spending distribution shows that a small number of customers generate disproportionately high revenue.

### Business Insight:
The business is highly dependent on high-value customers, making them a priority for retention efforts.

---

### 3. Low Engagement in Web/App Activity
Web and product view data shows that most customers have low interaction levels, while a small subset is highly active.

### Business Insight:
Low engagement customers represent a weak retention base and are more likely to churn due to inactivity.

---

### 4. Support Ticket Concentration
A small group of customers generates a high number of support tickets.

### Business Insight:
These customers likely face unresolved issues or friction, increasing their likelihood of churn.

---

### 5. Refund Behavior Signals Dissatisfaction
Refund analysis shows that while most customers rarely return products, a subset has consistently higher refund rates.

### Business Insight:
Refund-heavy customers represent dissatisfaction and are a high-risk churn segment.

---

### 6. Campaign Exposure is Uneven
Campaign history shows uneven marketing exposure across customers, with some receiving significantly more campaigns than others.

### Business Insight:
Campaign frequency alone is not enough; excessive or irrelevant campaigns may not improve retention and could reduce engagement.

---

### 7. Churn Distribution is Imbalanced
Churn labels show that non-churned customers dominate the dataset, while churned customers represent a smaller group.

### Business Insight:
This imbalance means churn is a minority but critical outcome. Targeted modeling is required rather than generic accuracy-based approaches.

---

## What the Company Should Investigate Before Retention Campaign

- Why most customers remain low-frequency buyers
- Why a small group drives most revenue
- Root causes behind high support ticket users
- Drivers of refund-heavy behavior
- Whether low engagement users can be reactivated effectively
- Whether current campaigns are actually improving retention or just increasing exposure

---

## Recommendation

Retention strategies should NOT be generic.

Instead, the company should:
- Focus on high-value customers showing early disengagement
- Investigate dissatisfaction signals (tickets + refunds)
- Improve engagement before increasing campaign volume

---

## Conclusion
The analysis shows that churn risk is strongly behavior-driven, especially through low engagement, low purchase frequency, support issues, and refund behavior. Retention efforts should be targeted, not mass-marketed.
